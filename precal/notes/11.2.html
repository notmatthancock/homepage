11.2 — Arithmetic Sequences

A sequence, $\{a_n\}$, is arithmetic if $$ a_n = a_{n-1} + d $$ where $d$ is a constant, called the common difference

For example, $$ 0, 3, 6, 9, 12, \ldots $$ is arithmetic with $d=3$, and $$ 4, -1, -6, -11, \ldots $$ is arithmetic with $d=-5$.

Using the definition of an arithmetic sequence, we can find $d$ using any two consecutive terms, i.e. $$ d = a_n - a_{n-1} $$
Which of the following are arithmetic? Find the common difference for those that are.
  1. $10, 20, 30, 40, \ldots$
  2. $2, 4, 8, 16, \ldots$
  3. $\displaystyle \frac{4}{3}, \frac{1}{3}, -\frac{2}{3}, -\frac{5}{3}, \ldots$
  1. Arithmetic, $d=10$.
  2. Not Arithmetic.
  3. Arithmetic, $d=-1$.

Explicit Formula for the $n$th term

So far, we've defined the arithmetic sequence recursively. However, it's not too difficult to come up with an explicit formula, using just the recursive definition: \begin{align*} a_n &= a_{n-1} + d \\ &= \left( a_{n-2} + d \right) + d \\ &= a_{n-2} + 2d \\ &= \left( a_{n-3} + d \right) + 2d \\ &= a_{n-3} + 3d \\ &= \vdots \\ &= a_1 + (n-1)d \\ \end{align*} Thus, we have an explicit formula for the $n$th term in an arithmetic series (which holds for all $n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$): $$ a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d $$
Find the 6th term of an arithmetic sequence whose first term, $a_1= \sqrt{2}$, and common difference, $d=-sqrt{2}$.
$-4\sqrt{2}$
Find the 45th term of the arithmetic sequence: $$ 1, \frac{1}{2}, 0, -\frac{1}{2}, \ldots $$
$-21$
Find the $n$th term of an arithmetic sequence if $a_1=3$, and $d=-1/3$.
$a_n = 3 - (n-1)/3 = \frac{10}{3} - \frac{n}{3}$
Given an arithmetic sequence with $a_{21} = 65$ and $a_{41} = 125$, find the initial term and the common difference.
$a_1=5$, $d=3$

Summing Arithmetic Sequences

Let's denote the sum of the first $n$ terms of a sequence by: $$ S_n = \sum_{j=1}^n a_j $$ If $a_j$ come from an arithmetic sequence, one can show that: \begin{align*} S_n &= \frac{n}{2}\left(a_1 + a_n\right) \\ &= n * \left( \text{average of first term and nth term} \right) \end{align*}
Given the arithmetic sequence, $\displaystyle \left\{1, \frac{1}{2}, 0, -\frac{1}{2}, \ldots \right\}$, find the sum, $S_{41}$.
$-369$
Find the sum of all the multiples of 7 between 21 and 119.
$1050$

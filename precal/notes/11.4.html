11.4 — Mathematical Induction

Suppose you observe an infinite amount of ducks in a row. You label them $1, 2, 3, \ldots$ and so on.

Next,
  1. you observe that the first duck is yellow, and
  2. you prove (somehow) that for any label, $n$, duck $n$ being yellow implies duck $n+1$ is yellow.
You conclude, therefore, that all ducks sitting in this row are yellow.

Formal Statement of First Principle of Mathematical Induction

Let $P(n)$ be a statement (which can be true or false) that depends on the value of $n$. The first principle of mathematical induction says that $P(n)$ is true for all $n=1,2,3,\ldots$ if we prove that
  1. $P(1)$ is true, and
  2. $P(n)$ is true implies $P(n+1)$ is true.
Step 1 is called the base case while step 2 is called the induction step. Step 2 could also be stated as $P(n+1)$ is true under the assumption that $P(n)$ is true.
To prove by induction that $n^2 + 7n -2$ is divisible by $2$ is true for all integers $n$, we assume that $k^2 + 7k -2$ is divisible by $2$ is true for some positive integer $k$, and we show that $k^2+7k-2+A$ is divisible by $2$ where $A$ is:
$A=2(k+4)$
To prove by induction that $n^2 - 7n -2$ is divisible by $2$ is true for all integers $n$, we assume that $k^2 - 7k -2$ is divisible by $2$ is true for some positive integer $k$, and we show that $A$ is divisible by $2$ where $A$ is:
$A = (k+1)^2 - 7(k+1) - 2$
To prove by induction that $1+3+5+\ldots+(2n-1) = n^2$ is true for all positive integers $n$, we assume $1+3+5+\ldots+(2k-1) = k^2$ is true for some positive integer $k$, and show that $1+3+5+\ldots+(2k-1)+(2(k+1)-1)=A$ where $A$ is:
$(k+1)^2$
To prove by induction that $11+7+3+\ldots+(15-4n)=13n-2n^2$ is true for all positive integers $n$, we assume $11+7+3+\ldots+(15-4k)=13k-2k^2$ is true for some positive integer $k$, and show that $11+7+3+\ldots+(15-4k)+A=13(k+1)-2(k+1)^2$ is true where $A$ is:
$11-4k$
Find $a_2$ and $a_3$ such that $\displaystyle 1+a_2+a_3+\ldots+a_n = \frac{3^n-1}{2}$ for all $n$.
$a_2 = 3$, $a_3 = 9$

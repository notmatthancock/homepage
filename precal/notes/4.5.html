4.5 — Logarithmic and Exponential Equations

We will encounter various forms of equations involving logarithms and exponential functions.

Form One

$$ a^u = a^v $$ If we can manipulate an equation into this form, then we can set $u = v$ and solve.
Solve for $x$: $\;\;16^{2-x} = 8$.
$5/4$
Solve for $x$: $\;\;e^{3-2x} = \frac{1}{e}$.
$2$

Form Two

$$ a^u = b^v $$ Suppose we wanted to solve for $u$. We could take the natural logarithms of both sides and manipulate the expressions to find what we're looking for: $$ \ln a^u = \ln b^v \rightarrow u\ln a = v\ln b \rightarrow u = \frac{v\ln b}{\ln a} \; \ldots $$
Solve for $x$: $\;\; 3^{x-1} = 4^{2-x}$
$\displaystyle \frac{\ln(48)}{\ln(12)}$
Solve for $x$: $\;\; e^{2x-3} = 4$
$\displaystyle \frac{3+\ln 4}{2}$

Form Three

$$ \log_a u = \log_a v $$ Similar to form one, we can set $u = v$ for this form due to the one-to-one property of logarithms.
Solve for $x$: $\;\; \displaystyle \frac{2}{3} \log_4x = \log_425$
$125$
Solve for $x$: $\;\; \ln x = \ln 12 - \ln(x+1)$
$3$

Form Four

$$ \log_a u = b $$ Using the definition of logarithms, we can transform this form into $a^b = u$.
Solve for $x$: $\;\; \displaystyle \log_x \frac{1}{32} = -2$
$\sqrt{32}$
Solve for $x$: $\;\; \displaystyle \log_{32} \left(\frac{1}{32} \right)^x = -2$
$2$
Solve for $x$: $\;\; \displaystyle \log_4(x+2) - \frac{1}{2}\log_4(x^2-4) = 1$
$34/15$

4.7 — Exponential Growth and Decay

If we say a substance / population grows or decays exponentially, this means that the amount at a time, $t$, is given by: $$ A(t) = A_0 e^{kt} $$ where $A_0 = A(0)$ is the initial amount, and $k$ is the growth / decay rate.

If $k \gt 0$ we call it exponential growth while if $k \lt 0 $, we call it exponential decay. This is also sometimes referred to as uninhibited growth / decay.
The size, $P$, of a certain insect population at time, $t$ (in days), obeys the function $P(t) = 350e^{0.04t}$. After how many days will the insect population reach 630?
$\displaystyle t = \frac{\ln\left(\frac{9}{5}\right)}{0.04}$
Salt decompses in water into sodium and chloride ions according to the law of uninhibited decay, $S(t) = S_0e^{kt}$, with $k \lt 0$. If the initial amount of salt is 30 kilograms, and after 4 hours, 5 kilograms of salt is left, how much salt is left after 8 hours?
$\displaystyle 30e^{-8\left(\frac{\ln 6}{4}\right)}$
The population, $N$ (in millions), of a country may be approximated by the formula, $N(t) = N_0e^{kt}$. If the population is 35 million initially and 45 million after 1 year, what will be the population in 2 years?
$\displaystyle 35e^{2\ln\left(\frac{9}{7}\right)}$
The population of a state is known to follow the exponential law. If the population increased from 300,000 in 2011 to 900,000 in 2014, what will be the population in 2031?
$\displaystyle 300,000e^{30 \left( \frac{\ln(9)}{3} \right)}$
Iodine-31 is a radioactive material that decays according to $A(t) = A_0e^{-0.029t}$, where $A_0$ is the initial amount present and $A(t)$ is the amount present at time, $t$ (in days). What is the half-life of iodine I-31?
$\displaystyle \frac{\ln 2}{0.029}$
Find the exponential function, $N(t) = N_0e^{kt}$, that satisfies the conditions, $N(0) = 12$ and $N(3) = 4$.
$\displaystyle N(t) = 12e^{(\frac{\ln\frac{1}{3}}{3})t}$

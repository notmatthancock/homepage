2.7 - Derivatives and Rates of Change

The derivative of a function a point $x=a$ is defined as: $$ f'(a) = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a} $$ or equivalently, $$ f'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} $$ $f'(a)$ is the slope of the tangent line of $f(x)$ passing through $(a,f(a))$.
Find the equation of the tangent line of $f(x)=x^2$ at $x=1$. (ans=factor the numerator)
  1. The average rate of change of $f(x)$ over $[a,b]$ is $\frac{f(a)-f(b)}{a-b}$
  2. The derivative of $f(x)$ at $x=a$ is the instantaneous rate of change of $f(x)$ with respect to $x$.

2.6 - Limits at Infinity; Horizontal Asymptotes

Let $f(x) = \frac{1}{1+e^{-x}}$. What happens when $x \to \infty$? When $x \to -\infty$?
If we can make $f(x)$ as close as we want to some value $L$ by making $x$ sufficiently large, we say: $$ \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = L $$ Limits as $x \to -\infty$ are defined analogously.

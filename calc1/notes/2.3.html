2.3 - Calculating limits using limit laws

Two special limits:
  1. $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} x = a$
  2. For a constant $c$, $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} c = c$

Theorem 2.3.1 (limit laws) Suppose $$ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \;\; \text{and} \;\; \lim_{x \to a} g(x) $$ both exist, then the following are true:
  1. $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} \left[ f(x) + g(x) \right] = \lim_{x \to a}f(x) + \lim_{x \to a}g(x)$
  2. $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} \left[ f(x) \cdot g(x) \right] = \lim_{x \to a}f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \to a}g(x)$
  3. If $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a}g(x) \neq 0$, $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} \left[ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \frac{\lim_{x \to a}f(x)}{\lim_{x \to a}g(x)}$


Corollaries to Theorem 2.3.1:
  1. Let $c$ be any constant and $g(x)=c$, then using Thm 2.3.1 Rule 2, $$\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} \left[ cf(x) \right] = c\lim_{x \to a}f(x)$$
  2. $$\begin{align*}\displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} \left[ f(x) - g(x) \right] &= \lim_{x \to a} \left[ f(x) + (-1)g(x) \right] \\ &= \lim_{x \to a} f(x) + \lim_{x \to a} \left[ (-1)g(x) \right] \tag{Thm 2.3.1 Rule 1} \\ &= \lim_{x \to a} f(x) - \lim_{x \to a} g(x) \tag{Corollary 1} \end{align*}$$
  3. Letting $g(x)=f(x)$, and repeatedly apply Thm 2.3.1 Rule 2, $$\lim_{x \to a} \left[ f(x)^n \right] = \left[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \right]^n$$
  4. Using the previous corollary, $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \lim_{x \to a} \left[ (\sqrt[n]{f(x)})^n \right] = \left[ \lim_{x \to a} \sqrt[n]{f(x)} \right]^n$. Taking $n$th roots, we have: $$\lim_{x \to a} \sqrt[n]{f(x)} = \sqrt[n]{ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) }$$
  5. Letting $f(x)=x$, we have two special cases of the previous two corollaries, $$\lim_{x \to a} \left[ x^n \right] = a^n \;\; \text{and} \;\; \lim_{x \to a} \sqrt[n]{x} = \sqrt[n]{a}$$
Note: Theorem 2.3.1 and its corollaries hold for one-sided limits also.

Theorem 2.3.2 (direct substitution property) If $f$ is a polynomial or a rational function and $a$ is in the domain of $f$, then $$ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a) $$ Foreshadowing: continuous functions are those which satisfy the direct substitution property
